Often access to material is severely limited, and the quality can vary from surgery to surgery. It also depends on why the surgery was does, as often there was an underlying condition being corrected, so for tissue to be removed, it would mean there may be some underlying problem with the neurons and associated cells. The other deterring factor is while we can get tissue, it has a finite lifespan, and can’t be expanded beyond what the starting material is. Stem cells at least alleviated a lot of these issues, we can grow a patients cells and make any cell type we want when we want, and we never have to worry about material being available. Yes it has its limitations, but it has opened up a lot of doors and possibilities that weren’t there ten years ago.
