Despite the heavy burden of the disease, most human infections are asymptomatic (afebrile) and can last for months/years. In regions where malaria is seasonal, such as in The Gambia, West Africa, it is thought that these asymptomatic infections act as a reservoir during the dry season when there is no or little transmission. Long-lasting infections could be turned to our advantage if we study them as a new host-pathogen interaction model, by regular sampling of an asymptomatic volunteer, investigating the parasite biology in its natural environment.
